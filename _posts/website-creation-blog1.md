Hello world!

After long efforts behind the scenes to learn a bit about Jekyll, Ruby, and Markdown, my Github Pages website is now live!

I feel like it was certainly worth the effort to learn a bit about the topic of static website development - and it has also increased my respect for those who work in website development. The template for the site, which I forked from https://github.com/academicpages/academicpages.github.io, is excellent and very customizable.
Perhaps in a future blog post, I can outline the steps I took to create a website on Github Pages using Jekyll for the curious user.

It's funny, though... now I have this website and I'm unsure what I want to do with it. I'm really not much of a social media user and so, it seems a bit weird to air my thoughts here as blog posts, even on my own website. Whereas many folks seem to be perfectly ok with engaging in technologically driven form of communication, especially in its most nefarious forms (i.e., Facebook, Instagram, LinkedIn), I have often felt that after a long day doing research, the last thing I want to do is engage on the computer or on my phone. I guess there is also that sinking suspicion that maybe no one cares what I think about certain topics... 

I'm going to just have to push that thought aside. What I hope to do is to post in longer formats about engaging in research, ecological theory, geospatial data science, and other topics are of interest to me in this journey. If someone else gets something out of these blog posts, I'll be very happy! At the very least, it'll give me a way to track the development of my thoughts on topics of interest.

Stay tuned!
